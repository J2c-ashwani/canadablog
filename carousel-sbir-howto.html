Check Eligibility First
Before writing a single word, confirm your business meets the basic SBIR requirements — or you'll waste weeks on a disqualified application.
✅
You need: US-based for-profit company, under 500 employees, majority owned by US citizens or permanent residents.

Step 2 of 5
Pick the Right Agency
There are 11 federal agencies running SBIR programs. Choosing the wrong one is the #1 mistake founders make. Match your tech to their mission.
🎯
Tech startups → NSF · DOE
Health & Biotech → NIH
Defence tech → DOD · DARPA
AgriTech → USDA

Step 3 of 5
Write Your Technical Narrative
This is where 80% of applicants fail. Reviewers score on two dimensions: Technical Merit AND Commercial Potential. You need to nail both.
💡
Frame every technical paragraph with a market outcome. Don't just say what it does — say what problem it solves and how large that market is.

Step 4 of 5
Submit Before the Deadline
SBIR submission portals close at the exact second of the deadline. Late submissions are rejected with zero exceptions — even by 1 minute.
⏰
Submit at least 72 hours early. Portal outages are common near deadlines. Register on grants.gov weeks in advance — takes 3-5 business days.
fsidigital.ca/usa/federal-grants

5
Step 5 of 5 🏆
Prepare for Phase II
If awarded Phase I ($275K), immediately begin building your Phase II application ($1.7M). The window is shorter than most think — just 6-12 months.
🚀
Phase II requires proven commercialisation traction: letters of intent from customers, pilot users, or private co-investment. Start collecting these on Day 1.
